**CITATION**: Hussein et al. (2025) - Comparative Effectiveness of Bariatric Surgery Versus GLP-1 Receptor Agonists in Reducing the Risk of New-Onset of NASH
**CONTEXT**:
- Sample: 180,022 adults (143,404 BS, 36,618 GLP-1 RA) with BMI ≥35, no history of NAFLD/NASH
- Setting: Retrospective multinational cohort from North America and Europe using TriNetX platform
- Period: January 2014 - December 2019, with follow-up periods of 1, 3, 5, and 7 years
**DATA**:
- Bariatric surgery vs GLP-1 RA hazard ratio for NASH: **HR 0.509 (95% CI 0.469-0.551)**
- Bariatric surgery vs GLP-1 RA hazard ratio for HCC: **HR 0.304 (95% CI 0.099-0.931)**
- Liver cirrhosis risk reduction: **HR 0.865 (95% CI 0.696-1.075)** (not statistically significant)
- After propensity matching: 33,594 patients in each group (mean age ~49 years, ~72% female)
- "BS was significantly associated with lower risk of new onset of NASH/NAFLD"
